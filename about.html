    About Our Institution

    The Church Centenary Memorial Industrial Training Institute was established in 1984, on the momentous occasion of the fourth centenary celebrations of the age-old pilgrim centre, St. Andrew’s Basilica, Arthunkal. Through its disciplined and dedicated efforts, the institution has always been able to empower the lives of several young persons of coastal Alappuzha through sustainable career and jobs for about four decades. It is directed by the Rector and Vicar of St. Andrew’s Basilica. The Bishop of Alappuzha will be the patron of the Institute. It imparts training according to the scheme of National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT) Govt. of India.

    Our mission:

    To train students in good technical skills and bring up disciplined, morally upright and above all socially conscious citizens.

    Our vision:

    To nurture technically skilled young persons to serve and empower the society.
